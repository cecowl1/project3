    SWISS DESIGN

    Shigeo Fukuda was influenced originally by Swiss style and design, often referred to as the International Typographic Style, is a design movement that emerged in Switzerland in the mid-20th century and has had a profound influence on the global design landscape. Swiss design is characterized by its emphasis on clarity, simplicity, and readability, Swiss graphic design is renowned for its meticulous attention to detail and precision.

    ILLUSIONISM

    As he began his career, he took particular interest in illusionist movements. Illusionism in graphic design refers to the intentional use of visual tricks, techniques, and optical illusions to create a sense of depth, movement, or perception that may not accurately represent reality. Graphic designers often employ illusionistic elements to capture attention, convey a particular mood, or enhance the overall visual impact of a design. His design style would use postive and negative spaces to develop interesting forms that make you question what you are seeing.

    PAUL RAND

    Paul Rand helped escalate Shigeo's career in graphic design. There is no evidence of him being influnced by Rand's design style but Rand took an interest in his work and invited him to America to do a gallery show. Shigeo Fukuda was able to share his love for scultpure and graphic design.
